Question

Which environmental variables contribute most to ecosystem risk under changing climatic conditions?

Good answer

The environmental factors that appear to contribute most to increasing ecosystem risk are overall vegetation productivity, potential species richness and the density of tree cover. Together, these variables account for almost all of the estimated contribution to ecosystem risk in the current analysis, suggesting that biological conditions are the strongest indicators of ecosystem vulnerability.

By contrast, recent changes in land imperviousness, tree cover, land use and grassland presence have shown little influence on the present risk assessment. Their limited contribution does not imply that these processes are ecologically unimportant, but rather that they are not among the principal drivers identified in the current model.

This interpretation is consistent with the available ecological evidence indicating that ecosystem risk arises from the combined effects of climatic variability, anthropogenic pressures and biodiversity rather than from any single environmental stressor.
